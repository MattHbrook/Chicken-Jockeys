What is a Velocity Plan?

A velocity plan is a crucial tool for software development teams that helps predict and manage project timelines based on the team's proven capacity to deliver work.

Just as Minecraft players need to plan their resources and time to build complex structures, development teams use velocity planning to:

  • Estimate how much work can be completed in upcoming sprints
  • Forecast delivery dates for features based on historical performance
  • Allocate resources effectively across multiple projects
  • Create realistic timelines that stakeholders can trust

THE PROBLEM

Current Challenges

Challenge Overview

Time Consumption

Teams waste valuable time on manual, error-prone processes for timeline estimation and status tracking.

Reliability Issues

Low confidence in estimates leads to frequent timeline extensions that frustrate stakeholders and damage credibility.

Communication Challenges

Different teams have inconsistent timeline data, making it difficult to align on expectations and dependencies.

Customer Dissatisfaction

Unreliable timelines lead to overpromising and underdelivering, creating unhappy customers and damaged relationships.

Financial Impact

Slipped timelines result in missed financial forecasts, affecting budgeting, resource allocation, and strategic planning.

Visibility Problems

Teams struggle to visualize the roadmap holistically, making it difficult to understand risks and dependencies across teams.

A properly implemented Velocity Planning tool would address these pain points by providing a centralized, data-driven approach to timeline forecasting that increases confidence, improves communication, and enables better strategic decision-making throughout the organization.

Current Process

Here is what a NetDocuments Engineering Manager does currently with the Excel-based velocity planning tool:

  • 1. Build the ADO query for the velocity sheet
  • 2. Run the query
  • 3. (MAC) Export the query as a CSV
  • 4. Import the CSV into Excel (copy and paste into custom Excel spreadsheet)
  • 5. Update values to be accurate (missing data, updating existing data, t-shirt sizes, costs, remaining story points per item)
  • 6. Export the sheet as a CSV
  • 7. Import the results into ADO
  • 8. Copy and paste the sheet into the master sheet
  • 9. Add colors and visual enhancements

Key Challenges

  • This is a time-consuming, manual, error-prone process for the Engineering Manager while trying to get a lot of other work completed
  • This is done on a weekly basis and is a significant time sink for our Engineering Managers
  • The entire organization has interest in this output and the output can vary from team to team
  • Dynamic, "What-If" planning conversations are difficult with this cumbersome approach to timeline planning
